Rune is a calm, fast notes app for people who don't want to trust an app with
their data. Your notes never leave your device, they are encrypted at rest, and
the app makes no network connections at all. There is no account, no cloud, and
no sign-up.

Privacy by construction, not by promise:

* No telemetry, no analytics, no crash reporting, no advertising, no tracking —
  none, not "anonymised", not "aggregated".
* No networking code. Put your device in airplane mode and everything still
  works. If anything ever tries to reach the network, that is a bug.
* No account and no server. There is no "we" that could hold your data.

Real encryption:

* Your passphrase derives a key with Argon2id; notes are sealed with
  XChaCha20-Poly1305 authenticated encryption. Only ciphertext is ever written
  to disk.
* The passphrase is never stored and is not recoverable. If you forget it, your
  notes are gone — no reset, no backdoor. That is the price of real privacy.
* A wrong passphrase simply cannot decrypt your notes.

What you can do:

* Create, edit, search, and organise plain-text notes with an Apple-Notes-style
  layout that adapts from phone to desktop.
* Auto-lock on inactivity and lock-on-background; the app starts locked.
* Export an encrypted backup (safe to keep anywhere); plaintext export is gated
  behind an explicit warning.
* Capture voice notes — recording stays entirely on your device.

Free and open source under the GNU GPLv3. The security-critical code is a small,
dependency-light, pure-Dart core so it can be read and audited. Releases ship
with cryptographic provenance (SLSA build-provenance attestations and a keyless
cosign signature) and are built reproducibly, so you can verify the binary was
built from this source.

This is honest software: it tells you what it does not yet do. See the project's
SECURITY.md for the threat model and current limitations.
